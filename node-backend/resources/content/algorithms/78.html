Buffer - Temporarily holding spot while data is being transferred from one place to another
  •    Intentionally limited in size
Streams - A sequence of data made available over time
  • Eventually combined together as a whole
  • So it enables data to be broken down into parts to be streamed
A good real world application is when we are "streaming" movies, a bit of data is processed in a buffer first and then processed to display. 
For example 10 mins of the video is incoming from a stream, processed in the buffer and then displayed to you. while the next 10 mins of the video is also incoming buffered.

Pipes - Connecting two streams to another, say from a readable stream, connects to a writable stream. 
  • A real world application of this could be when you are downloading a file.
